Portfolio for Project 1 in DP1, year 2 of Bartlett MEng course (Uploaded: 21/07/21)

This project took place across the first term of our first design studio module in year 2. It was quite a challenging module and we were thrown in the deep end, especially in this year of online study. Our brief was to design an aparatus at Dugneness beach that can harness, expose, highlight or draw inspiration from the surrounding landscape and elements, especially Derek Jarman's Prosepct Cottage. Being quite a vague brief, I enjoyed the experimental and open-ended nature of the project and felt I was able to explore and develop new skills and ideas. My aparatus was a 'salt garden' that collects seawater through capilary action and forms salt crystals on a glass structure. It would grow dynamically and sporadically with rising sea levels, seasonal variations in temperature and humidity and interaction with shingle and plants.
